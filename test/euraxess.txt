Offer Description

A research assistant position in physical chemistry is available at the J. Heyrovsky Institute of Physical Chemistry in Prague in a close collaboration with the University of Chemistry and Technology. The research project concerns inducing chemical changes in solutions of organic compounds by impact of free electrons on these. The solutions will be in the form of liquid microjets in vacuum. The researcher will be responsible for development and practical implementation of the experiments, and for the analysis of the induced changes and chemical modifications. The position is initially open for six months with a possibility of prolongation. 

Essential skills and qualifications

Competence in organic synthesis, photochemical and analytical techniques. Especially desirable is hands-on experience with UV-VIS spectroscopy, fluorescence spectroscopy and HPLC-MS. Experience with nucleic acid self-assembling nanostructures is an advantage.
